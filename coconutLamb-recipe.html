Grenadian Coconut Lamb Curry

Made with substantial goat flesh cooked till extra soft, fresh spices, and so much flavor, this rich and satisfyingly delicious Curry Goat is best served with rice and peas.Take a bite out of the Islands !

People… Curry goat is without a doubt one of my favorite West Indian meals. Curry, in general, is one of my favorite types of spices. This is a dish that I grew up with and have enjoyed for a long time!

To begin, curry goat is a delicious and filling stew prepared with robust goat meat and curry powder. Aromatics, various spices, and potatoes are all common ingredients. Curry goat is also cooked for a few hours to ensure that the goat flesh is totally soft. It practically crumbles and absorbs all of the wonderful flavors it was cooked in. This meal is popular in the Caribbean, India, and Southeast Asia.

A decent pot of curry goat should always be served with rice and peas, according to any Islander! Aside from being a part of my culture, I enjoy how comfortable this meal is. The gravy is exceptionally thick and delicious, and it has a lot of flavor. The goat flesh is fork-tender, and the potatoes offer a layer of comfort.

prep time

30 mins

cook time

3 hours

servings

6 servings

Tags: Beef Oil-down Pelau Breakfast

Instructions

Step 1

Over high heat, heat you pot

Step 2

When the pot is hot, add the oil, followed by the brown sugar.

Step 3

When the sugar starts to bubble and caramelize, stir constantly with a pot spoon until it turns a rich amber color with a hint of black jack.

Step 4

Add the seasoned chicken pieces to the pot right away and toss to uniformly coat them in caramelized sugar. Allow 10 minutes for the chicken to simmer (brown).

Step 5

Reduce to medium heat, add the rice, and completely combine with the chicken. Allow 4–5 minutes for the rice to parch or brown with the chicken to produce a nutty taste and caramel color.

Step 6

Add the pigeon peas, then the garlic, onions, chives, pimentos, and pumpkin.

Step 7

Combine the coconut powder and water in the pot. The rice should be completely submerged in water. Stir in the aromatics, then add the entire pepper, cover, and cook on a low heat. (Approximately 8–10 minutes)

Step 8

Remove the lid after this time and add the carrots and corn. Stir to incorporate, and at this stage, check the rice for doneness. If it's still a touch hard or near to being done, add a little more water and cover the pot to simmer for a few minutes longer. However, if the Pelau is still damp and near to being done, keep it uncovered to enable the extra water to evaporate.

Step 9

Season with salt and pepper after adding the chadon beni and salted butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and stir well to combine all ingredients, preventing the bottom from burning.

Step 10

Carry on like this until you've achieved the required uniformity.

Step 11

Allow 15 minutes for the flavors to merge and set after removing from the heat.

Step 12

Serve on its own or with coleslaw for a delicious side dish.

Ingredients

3 Tbsp Vegetable Oil

½ Cup Brown Sugar

1 Whole Seasoned Chicken (Cut into Pieces)

2¼ Cups Rice

1 Tin of Pigeon Peas

6 Cloves of Garlic (Finely Minced)

½ Cup Onions (Finely Minced)

2 Bundles Chives (Chopped)

6 Pimento Peppers (Finely Minced)

1¼ Cup Pumpkin (Cubed)

¼ Cup Coconut Milk

Water (About 4 Cups)

1 Whole Scotch Bonnet Pepper

¾ Cup Carrots (Cubed)

½ Cup Corn Kernels (Optional)

½ Cup Corn Kernels (Optional)

2 Bundles Chadon Beni/Culantro (Finely Chopped)

Salt and Black Pepper to taste

Tools

Hand Blend

Large Pot

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
