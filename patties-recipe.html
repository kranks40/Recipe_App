Jamaican Beef Patties

This Jamaican Beef Patties recipe will transport you to the Caribbean. To give it that real golden color and delectable crust, add turmeric and curry powder to homemade burger pastry.

Packed with the greatest hot minced beef patty filling from Jamaica. Cheese is not required. Make large or little versions that may be prepared ahead of time and reheated. Alternatively, freeze for a nice snack later.

prep time

20 mins

cook time

35 mins

servings

5 servings

Jamaican patties have a flaky crusted golden pastry, filled with a tasty spicy savoury meat filling. They originate from Jamaica, influenced by a mix of Cornish, Indian & African cuisines.

Jamaican Beef Patties are made with a simple pastry that may be made at home. It's a simple shortcrust pastry with the addition of Curry Powder and Tumeric (we used Garam Masala). As a result, the spices give the patty pastry a lovely golden color and a delicious flavor!

Instructions

Pastry

Step 1

Put all Pastry ingredients (except the water) in a food processor and tap the pulse button to slowly incorporate the butter into the flour. If doing by hand rub butter into flour until it represents fine breadcrumbs, try to work as quick as possible to avoid butter melting

Step 2

Add ice-cold water bit by bit and knead with hands into the dough until it forms a playdoh like texture. (you may need a little less/more water than stated above) Try not to handle to much.

Step 3

Place pastry covered in the fridge whilst you create the beef filling.

Beef mince patty filling

Step 1

Heat oil in pan and add onions and (bell) red peppers sautee for 5 minutes until soft, add spring onions.

Step 2

Add mince, garlic, chilli, paprika, garam masala, turmeric and salt. Stir through until mince has browned

Step 3

Finally add dried/fresh thyme and stock. Place lid on and leave to simmer for 15 minutes whilst you cut out pastry.

Create Patties

Step 1

Preheat oven to 190Â°C (fan)/ 375F Remove pastry from fridge, flour surface and roll out very thin.

Step 2

Use a saucer/small plate (about 17cm diameter) to cut around the pastry. Re-roll remaining pastry until all used up, you should have 5-6 circles. Or you could use smaller plates and create smaller patties?!

Step 3

Now put rolled out circles on to there own bit of parchment paper and add 2-3 tbsp of beef mixture in the middle

Step 4

Use finger to dap the outer circle with water

Step 5

Fold pastry over the mince and use a fork to crimp the edges together

Step 6

Whisk egg with water and brush on pastry to glaze. For a flat pastry fork a hole in the top. For a puffed-up pastry (as I like them) leave out the hole!

Step 7

Place all completed patties on to a tray (or two) and cook in the 190Â°C (fan)/ 375F oven for 20-25 minutes.

Step 8

Serve and enjoy!
