Stew Chicken

Grenadian chicken stew is a popular Caribbean meal that originated in Grenada. Chicken legs, chicken stock, ketchup, onions, garlic, bell peppers, carrots, sugar, salt, pepper, curry powder, oregano, and paprika powder are commonly used.

Season the chicken, carrots, onions, bell peppers, and onions with paprika, oregano, salt, pepper, and curry powder before braising in sugar and simmering in chicken stock and ketchup. The stew is let to boil until the chicken is soft enough to fall off the bones.

prep time

15 mins

cook time

65 mins

servings

2-4 servings

Tags: Bell Pepper Carrot Chicken Chicken Stock

This stew is usually topped with coriander and served as is or over white rice once it's finished. A glass of sorrel juice is advised to accompany the meal.

Growing up, we ate brown stewed chicken frequently, and it rapidly became one of my favorite stew dishes. In our family, it was on a weekly rotation, and we ate it more often than we ate these incredible oxtails.

Brown stew chicken takes a little longer to prepare than most other stew recipes. The meat is rinsed before being marinated in a "seasoning" made up of chopped aromatics and a few dry seasonings. The meat is then briefly browned on the stove top or in the oven before being stewed for 30-40 minutes, or until it is fall-apart tender.

I deviated from my mother's recipe by using boneless and skinless chicken, primarily because I dislike the texture of chicken skin in stew. You could use skinned and boned chicken, but anticipate it to take longer to cook.

Instructions

Step 1

Over high heat, heat you pot

Step 2

Sprinkle on curry powder, smoked paprika, oregano, kosher salt and pepper. Stir to evenly coat the whole mixture in seasoning.

Step 3

In a large pan (I used my 3.5 quart braiser pan) add sugar over medium high heat. Keep an eye on the sugar and watch it start to melt and slowly turn from clear to golden brown to dark amber brown. You have to keep your eye on the sugar as it can go from perfectly caramelized to burnt in a few seconds.

Step 4

Once the sugar is dark amber in color (think the color of maple syrup) add in the chicken and just enough chicken stock to reach the top of the chicken without totally covering it.

Step 5

Stir in ketchup, cover with a lid and let simmer over medium heat until the chicken is fall off the bone tender and fully cooked, about an hour. The mixture will cook down so if it starts to get dry and the chicken is not fully cooked simply add a little more stock.

Step 6

Once full cooked sprinkle with cilantro and serve plain or overtop rice.

Ingredients

6 chicken legs, skin removed

3 carrots, diced

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 yellow onion, diced

1 red bell pepper, diced

2 teaspoons curry powder

1 teaspoon smoked paprika

1/2 teaspoon oregano

Kosher salt and pepper, to taste

2 tablespoons sugar

2-3 cups chicken stock

1 tablespoon ketchup

2 tablespoons chopped cilantro, for topping (optional)

Tools

Large Bowl

Large Frying Pan

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
