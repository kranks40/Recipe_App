Grenadian Oil-down

Grenada, with its magnificent beaches, blue and crystalline water, Caribbean Sea paradise, and its famed oil down!

Grenada is also known as the "Island of Spice" because it has more spices per square mile than any other country in the world. Grenada is a Caribbean island located less than 100 miles north of Venezuela in the southern Caribbean. Grenada is the major island, followed by Carriacou and Petite Martinique. It is a sovereign republic with three islands.

prep time

10 mins

cook time

1 hr

servings

6 servings

The fragrance of spices pervades the country, earning it the nickname "island of delights." It is known for its cinnamon, other spices, chocolate, vanilla, ginger, mace, and nutmeg-scented woodlands.

Grenada produces a third of the world's nutmeg. Before a cyclone devastated most of the plantations, it was the largest producer. Grenada is now the world's second-largest nutmeg grower, after Guatemala. All of this, with the gorgeous turquoise waters and white sand...

The oil down is without a doubt the meal that best represents Grenadian cuisine. This is the island's traditional cuisine, a stew of cod, herring, or salted pork stewed in coconut milk with breadfruit and taro leaves, to name a few ingredients. The term stems from the fact that the meal is cooked until all of the coconut milk has been absorbed and only coconut oil remains at the bottom.

What about taro leaves and breadfruit if coconut, its cream, oil, and milk have no secrets for 196 flavors?

Instructions

Step 1

Cut the cod into large pieces and place in a colander.

Step 2

Immerse the colander into a large container filled with water and put it in the refrigerator.

Step 3

Desalt for 24 hours, changing the water as often as possible.

Step 4

In a cast iron pan preferably, sauté the onions over medium-low heat.

Step 5

Add the hot pepper, garlic, chives, ginger, thyme, and parsley and cook for one minute, stirring constantly.

Step 6

Add the breadfruit, carrots, green bell pepper, celery and taro leaves.

Step 7

Mix well and cook for 5 minutes over medium / high heat.

Step 8

Add coconut milk, heavy cream, nutmeg and turmeric.

Step 9

Carefully place the fish in the sauce.

Step 10

Add salt and pepper.

Step 11

Cook for 50 minutes total. 25 minutes over medium heat and 25 minutes over low heat until sauce is reduced.

Ingredients

2 lb salted cod , cut into chunks

1 large breadfruit , peeled and cut into pieces (or 2 potatoes)

½ lb taro leaves (or spinach leaves), chopped

2 stalks celery

3 carrots , sliced

1 green bell pepper , finely chopped

3 onions , sliced

2 cloves garlic , crushed

2 red hot peppers , thinly diced

2 sprigs thyme

A few stems of chives , finely chopped

1 teaspoon turmeric

1 tablespoon fresh ginger , finely grated

½ teaspoon nutmeg

2 tablespoons fresh parsley , finely chopped

½ cup coconut milk

1 cup heavy cream

Salt

Pepper

5 tablespoons canola oil
